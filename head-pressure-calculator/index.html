FAQ
Use P (bar) = H (m) × density (kg/m³) × 9.81 / 100,000. For water at 20°C: P = H × 0.09791 bar.
Fluid head H is the equivalent height of a fluid column producing a given pressure. H = P / (ρ × g). Pump curves use metres of head because head is independent of fluid density.
Fluid density changes with temperature. Water at 20°C is 998.2 kg/m³ but at 80°C only 971.8 kg/m³ — a 2.6% difference that directly affects head-pressure conversion accuracy.
Specific gravity (SG) is fluid density relative to water at 4°C (1000 kg/m³). SG = ρ / 1000. The calculator lets you override density or SG directly for custom fluids.
